Off-cycle and OE.

The core primitive is simple: ask the employer when they want coverage to begin. They can choose when to send invites. That's the extent of their control. Everything else is logic we own. The same 35-day window runs year-round, for new hires and open enrollment alike.

30 days marketed window
+5 days last chance
= 35d total
Across all 365 invite dates, that window produces:
17 dates with 1 CSD Nov 9–25 · 35d window closes before Dec 29
308 dates with 2 CSDs standard — backstop if member misses first cutoff
40 dates with 3 CSDs window spans two cutoffs near EOM
Open enrollment runs Nov 9–Dec 28 on the same 30+5d window. Employers choose when to send invites. The Jan 4–8 extension is a deliberate tradeoff: it gives Nov 9–25 invites a second CSD, but introduces a named exception to the one-system model.
